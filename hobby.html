Philosophy: My Ultimate Passion

If there's one thing that captivates me more than anything else, it's philosophy. The search for deeper understanding, the exploration of human nature, and the questioning of existence—these ideas fascinate me endlessly. Philosophy isn't just about abstract thinking; it's a tool for navigating power, ethics, psychology, and life itself.

Books That Have Shaped My Thinking

Each book I read offers a new perspective, challenging my worldview and refining my thought process. Here are some of the most influential ones:

The 48 Laws of Power
Robert Greene
A guide to power dynamics, strategy, and manipulation, using historical examples.
🔹 "Never appear too perfect."
Being and Nothingness
Jean-Paul Sartre
Sartre’s existentialist masterpiece on consciousness, freedom, and the meaning of being.
🔹 "Man is condemned to be free."
Crime and Punishment
Fyodor Dostoevsky
A psychological exploration of guilt, morality, and redemption.
🔹 "Pain and suffering are always inevitable for a large intelligence and a deep heart."
Cows, Pigs, Wars, and Witches
Marvin Harris
An anthropological study of how cultural taboos shape societies.
🔹 "Culture is not random; it is deeply connected to survival."
Mini Philosophy: A Small Book of Big Ideas
Jonny Thomson
Simplifies major philosophical concepts for easy understanding.
🔹 "The unexamined life is not worth living." – Socrates
Principles for Dealing with the Changing World Order
Ray Dalio
Insights on economic cycles and global power shifts.
🔹 "History has proven that the biggest risks often come from things we least expect."
Principles for Success
Ray Dalio
A condensed version of Dalio’s approach to achieving goals.
🔹 "Pain + Reflection = Progress."
The 33 Strategies of War
Robert Greene
A tactical guide to conflicts—whether in war, business, or life.
🔹 "Appear weak when you are strong, and strong when you are weak." – Sun Tzu
The Future of an Illusion
Sigmund Freud
A critique of religion, arguing it is a psychological construct to deal with uncertainty.
🔹 "Religious doctrines are illusions, fulfillments of the oldest, strongest, and most urgent wishes of mankind."
Sometimes You Can Accomplish Most by Doing Nothing
S.J. Scott
Explores the power of patience and inaction.
🔹 "The art of doing nothing is doing it at the right time."
The Art of Living a Meaningless Existence
Philosophy of Nihilism
A deep dive into nihilism and how to find meaning despite it.
🔹 "Meaning is not found, it is created."
The Art of Love
Ovid
A classic Roman text on love, romance, and seduction.
🔹 "If you want to be loved, be lovable."
The Game of Life and How to Play It
Florence Scovel Shinn
A spiritual self-help book on faith and belief.
🔹 "Your word is your wand. The words you speak create your own destiny."
The Prince & The Art of War
Niccolò Machiavelli
A pragmatic approach to politics, power, and military strategy.
🔹 "It is better to be feared than loved, if you cannot be both."
The Art of Seduction
Robert Greene
An exploration of the psychology of attraction and influence.
🔹 "Powerful seducers are those who can make others feel strong and valued."
The Laws of Human Nature
Robert Greene
A study of human behavior and social dynamics.
🔹 "The most dangerous people are those who appear the most harmless."
Fascination with Debires: The Greek God

Among the many mythologies, Greek mythology stands out to me, and Debires is particularly intriguing. His essence represents mystery, unseen influence, and the balance between fate and free will. Unlike the Olympian gods, Debires operates in the shadows, subtly shaping destinies rather than directly intervening.

His existence raises a compelling question: Do we truly have control over our lives, or are we merely actors in a script written by forces beyond our comprehension?
